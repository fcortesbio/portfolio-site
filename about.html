About Me

From biology labs to backend systems - my journey in problem-solving and analytical thinking.

The Journey

My path to backend development began in an unexpected place - the world of biology. With a background in biological sciences, I developed a deep appreciation for systematic thinking, data analysis, and understanding complex interconnected systems.

The transition from studying biological systems to building digital ones felt natural. Both require careful observation, hypothesis testing, and the ability to break down complex problems into manageable components. The analytical mindset I developed in research has become invaluable in architecting scalable backend solutions.

Current Focus

Today, I work at TaskUs while continuously expanding my backend development skills. I'm passionate about building robust APIs, optimizing database performance, and creating systems that can scale efficiently. My approach combines the methodical thinking from my scientific background with modern development practices.

I believe in writing clean, maintainable code and documenting everything thoroughly. Whether it's implementing JWT authentication, designing RESTful APIs, or containerizing applications with Docker, I approach each challenge with curiosity and attention to detail.

Philosophy

"I don't know everything, but I'm obsessed with improving."

This mindset drives my continuous learning journey. From configuring Arch Linux to mastering Vim, from understanding database indexing to implementing microservice architectures - I embrace challenges that push me to grow as a developer.

I believe the best backend systems are those that work seamlessly behind the scenes, handling complexity gracefully while providing reliable, fast, and secure services to users and other systems.
